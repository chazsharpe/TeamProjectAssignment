Mathematics

Mathematics serves as a fundamental basis for most programs, providing the necessary tools for solving complex computational problems efficiently and accurately. Mathematical concepts such as geometry and trigonometry play a crucial role in computer graphics, particularly in rendering shapes and creating realistic images in video games and animations. Additionally, more basic mathematical operations, such as arithmetic and algebra, are extensively used in everyday algorithms for tasks such as data manipulation, sorting, searching, and optimization.
Module topics include:

Discrete Mathematics

Calculus

Linear Algebra

Probability and Statistics

Number Theory and Cryptography

Games Design

Game design is a captivating aspect of computer science that blends creativity, technology, and problem-solving. It involves crafting immersive worlds, characters, and gameplay mechanics to create engaging experiences. Beyond just playing games, understanding game design means delving into how games are constructed from the ground up. Whether you aspire to develop your own game or work for a major game studio, studying game design offers exciting opportunities to merge your passion for technology with your love of gaming.
Module topics include:

Game Mechanics

User Interface (UI) Design

Script Development

Computer Graphics

Ethical and Social Issues

Narratives, World Creation and Level Design
